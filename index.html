2

Calculate λ (Arrival Rate) and μ (Service Rate)

from raw data

▶ λ = Arrival Rate

λ = Customers Observed ÷ Observation Hours
Sunday: 43 ÷ (161/60) = 16.02 customers/hr
Friday: 32 ÷ (141/60) = 13.62 customers/hr

Sunday is ~18% busier than Friday.

▶ μ = Service Rate (per cashier)

First, average service time per customer:

Avg Service = Total Service Min ÷ # Customers
Sunday: 87.9/43 = 2.04 min → μ = 60/2.04/2 = 20.45/hr
Friday: 86.25/32 = 2.70 min → μ = 60/2.70/2 = 20.00/hr
⚙ Summary: Sunday: λ = 16.02/hr, μ = 20.45/hr  |  Friday: λ = 13.62/hr, μ = 20.00/hr  |  Currently 2 cashiers
3

Set Cost Assumptions

realistic Cambodia 2026 values
$ Server Cost = $8/hr per cashier

Cambodia 2026 minimum wage: $210/month (garment sector). Retail cashier jobs pay above minimum. Realistic estimate: $8/hr.

Source: Xinhua News, Sept 2025 — tradingeconomics.com/cambodia/minimum-wages
$ Waiting Cost = $5/hr per customer

Research: customers abandon queues after ~8 minutes. Average mart basket: ~$5. If 1 customer/hr leaves, that is $5 lost.

Source: ScanQueue 2026 — scanqueue.com/blog/state-of-customer-waiting-2026
Total Cost Formula
Total Cost = s × $8 + L × $5

where s = number of cashiers, L = average customers in system (from M/M/s model)

4

Apply M/M/s Queueing Model

the core math
◆ M/M/s Formulas

1. Utilization (ρ)

ρ = λ / (s × μ)

Must be < 1 or queue grows forever.

s=1: 16.02/(1×20.45)=0.784  |  s=2: 16.02/(2×20.45)=0.392

2. Empty Probability (P₀)

P₀ = 1 / [Σ(λ/μ)n/n! + (λ/μ)s/(s!(1-ρ))]
s=2: P₀ = 43.7%

3. Queue Length (Lq)

Lq = P₀ × (λ/μ)s × ρ / [s!(1-ρ)2]
s=2: Lq = 0.14 customers

4. Customers in System (L)

L = Lq + λ/μ
s=2: L = 0.14 + 0.78 = 0.93

5. Queue Wait Time (Wq) — Little's Law

Wq = Lq / λ
s=2: 0.14/16.02 = 0.53 min = 32 sec

6. Total Time in System (W)

W = Wq + 1/μ
s=2: 0.53 + 2.93 = 3.47 min
Why these formulas? They come from the M/M/s queueing model taught in ITL304. The model assumes Poisson arrivals and exponential service times (both "memoryless" M).
